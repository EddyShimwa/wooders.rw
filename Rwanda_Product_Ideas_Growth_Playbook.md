# Wooders Rwanda Product Expansion Playbook

This file focuses on **additional** product categories and wooden ideas you can implement in Rwanda to increase revenue across high-class, middle-class, and volume/value segments.

## 1) Reality-Checked Market Signals (Rwanda)

These recommendations are based on demand environments that are already visible in Rwanda:
- Rwanda tourism reached **USD 647M** in 2024, with **USD 84.8M from MICE** and **52,000+ delegates** from 115 events (RDB).
- Rwanda continues positioning itself as a meetings/events destination, with strong conference infrastructure (RCB + KCC ecosystem).
- Rwanda real GDP grew **8.9%** in 2024, with services regaining the largest employer position (World Bank).
- Household consumption and living standards continue improving (NISR EICV7 indicators).
- Digital and mobile behavior is strong enough to support WhatsApp-led selling and QR/NFC-assisted products (DataReportal).

Bottom line: hospitality, events, business branding, and practical home organization are good demand pools right now.

## 2) New Product Categories (Beyond Current Backlog)

## A) Real Estate & Apartment Fit-Out (Middle + Upper Middle)
- Entryway bench + shoe cabinet combos (compact Kigali apartments).
- Built-in closet drawer dividers and valet trays.
- TV console cable-hiding boxes.
- Balcony fold-down wall tables.
- Under-sink organizers and cleaning-supply racks.

Why this works:
- Recurring demand from renters/homeowners who want space efficiency, not only decor.
- Strong upsell path: from one organizer item to full-room fit-out.

## B) Kids / Family Utility (Middle Class Volume)
- Montessori-inspired learning towers and step stools.
- Toy storage chests and stackable book bins.
- Child study desks with adjustable legs.
- Name plaques for nursery/bedroom doors.

Why this works:
- Practical products with high repeat/referral potential among families.
- Easy bundling into "room starter packs."

## C) Faith & Community Institutions (Stable Volume)
- Portable lecterns/pulpits for churches and schools.
- Donation/offering boxes with lockable compartments.
- Choir/book stands and scripture display stands.
- Church/school recognition boards.

Why this works:
- Rwanda has strong church and school communities with predictable procurement cycles.
- B2B/institution sales can generate larger repeat orders.

## D) Corporate Branding & Office Utility (High Margin B2B)
- Boardroom table caddies (notepads, remotes, pens, tissues).
- Executive desktop sets (pen dock + phone stand + card holder).
- Reception queue signage systems.
- Meeting-room reserved signs and desk occupancy signs.
- Corporate onboarding gift boxes (wood base + branded insert).

Why this works:
- Businesses pay premiums for branded, professional presentation.
- Custom engraving/branding lifts margin.

## E) Event Production Products (Wedding + Corporate Events)
- Reusable backdrop panels and plinths.
- Aisle signage stands and welcome boards.
- Photo booth prop walls and premium frame stations.
- Ring bearer boxes and vow book stands.
- Event centerpieces (rent/sell hybrid model).

Why this works:
- Event ecosystem in Kigali and secondary cities keeps demand recurring.
- Rental + sales model creates two revenue streams from same inventory.

## F) Premium Hospitality Guest-Room Accessories (High Class)
- In-room luggage racks (wood + fabric strap variants).
- Bedside guest utility trays (keys/cards/watch).
- Amenity display boxes for tea/coffee setup.
- Do-not-disturb / clean-room door signs.
- Executive suite tissue and remote caddies.

Why this works:
- Hotels and lodges need cohesive room aesthetics and replacement cycles.
- Standardized SKUs make bulk production possible.

## G) Retail Merchandising Systems (Boutiques / Pharmacies / Beauty)
- Modular countertop display cubes and risers.
- Sliding drawer POS stands (small-footprint tills).
- Product category sign holders.
- Promotional end-cap mini stands.

Why this works:
- Retailers constantly update product presentation.
- Fast-turn B2B contracts with simple, repeatable fabrication.

## H) Outdoor / Garden Micro-Line (Middle + Upper Middle)
- Weather-sealed planter benches.
- Vertical herb-garden wall racks.
- Balcony plant ladder stands.
- Garden hose/utility storage boxes.

Why this works:
- Lifestyle + practical utility products with strong Instagram/visual appeal.
- Good attachment sales with home decor line.

## 3) Monetization Ladder (Serve All Income Segments)

Use a three-tier catalog so one workshop serves multiple classes without diluting brand:

1. `Essential` (high turnover)
- Typical range: `RWF 8,000 - 35,000`
- Products: small organizers, stands, signage, coasters, hooks
- Goal: cash flow + customer acquisition

2. `Core` (best margin/volume balance)
- Typical range: `RWF 35,000 - 180,000`
- Products: shelves, caddies, frames, trays, medium furniture accessories
- Goal: steady profitability and repeat buyers

3. `Signature` (premium/high class)
- Typical range: `RWF 180,000+`
- Products: hotel room packages, custom office sets, event installations, carved statement pieces
- Goal: high-margin projects and brand prestige

## 4) Highest-Probability New SKUs to Start With

If your goal is fastest income lift with manageable complexity:
1. Entryway bench + shoe cabinet
2. Modular countertop risers for boutiques/salons
3. Executive desk set (custom branded)
4. Portable lectern/pulpit
5. Learning tower (kids)
6. Event signage stands (rent + sale)
7. In-room guest utility trays for hotels
8. Amenity display boxes for lodges/hotels
9. Donation/offering lockbox
10. Vertical herb-garden wall rack

## 5) Growth, Ads, Search, and Outbound Angles

## Growth-Hacker Plays
- Bundle strategy: "Home Entry Set", "Cafe Starter Kit", "Hotel Room Pack", "Church Podium Pack".
- Repeat-order engine: reorder reminders for businesses every 60-90 days on consumable/replaceable pieces.
- Referral incentive: client gets discount on next order for each referred business.
- Rental-to-buy event model: apply 40-60% of rental fee toward purchase.

## Ad-Creative Angles
- High class angle: "Rwandan craftsmanship for premium spaces."
- Middle class angle: "Space-saving wood solutions built for Kigali homes."
- B2B angle: "Brand your business space in custom wood, made locally."
- Trust angle: "Designed and built in Rwanda, with direct WhatsApp support."

## Outbound Targets (Signal-Based)
Prioritize account lists by immediate procurement likelihood:
1. New/renovating cafes and restaurants
2. Hotels, lodges, and serviced apartments
3. Event planners and wedding venues
4. Salons, spas, and boutiques
5. Churches and private schools
6. Corporate offices and co-working spaces

Outbound message opening pattern:
- Trigger + relevance + visual sample + low-friction CTA.
- Example: "Saw your new venue launch in Kigali. We build branded wood table signage, menu stands, and checkout folders locally. Want a 1-page catalog and sample pricing?"

## 6) Reality-Checker: What to Avoid for Now

Avoid or delay these until you have stronger operational capacity:
- Heavy electronics-dependent products needing advanced after-sales support.
- Highly complex custom jobs with low repeatability and no standard parts.
- Very bulky low-margin items that are expensive to deliver and hard to scale.
- Product lines without a clear repeat buyer segment.

Rule: prioritize SKUs that are easy to standardize, easy to photograph, easy to explain on WhatsApp, and easy to reorder.
